Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 18, 2026

Short version: Nearfolk stores everything you enter — contacts, notes, settings, photos you attach — only on your device. Nothing is sent to us or anyone else. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no third-party trackers.

What Nearfolk stores

When you add a person to Nearfolk, the app saves their name, relationship, birthday, notes, the photo you optionally attach, and how often you'd like to be reminded to catch up. All of this is written to local storage on your device using Apple's standard app-storage APIs.

Permissions Nearfolk asks for

  • Contacts — only if you tap "Import from Contacts." Nearfolk reads your address book so you can pick people to add. The full address book is never copied or transmitted; only the contacts you explicitly select are saved into the app.
  • Photos / Camera — only if you attach a picture to a contact. The image is copied into the app's private storage on your device. It is not uploaded.
  • Notifications — only if you turn on push reminders. Reminders are scheduled locally on your device using iOS's built-in notification system. No reminder content is sent to any server.

What Nearfolk does not do

  • No account creation, no sign-in, no email collection.
  • No analytics, telemetry, crash reporting, or advertising SDKs.
  • No selling, sharing, or transmitting your data to third parties.
  • No tracking across apps or websites.

Exporting and deleting your data

You can export everything Nearfolk stores at any time from Settings → Export my data. The export is a JSON file containing your contacts, settings, and theme. To delete everything, uninstall the app — iOS removes all local app storage with it.

Children

Nearfolk is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any data about anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If Nearfolk's data practices ever change, this page will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change. Material changes will also be noted in the app.
